Chapter 01

AI Fundamentals

Understanding the building blocks of artificial intelligence — from its history to the core concepts that power modern systems.

1950

The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes the question "Can machines think?" and introduces the Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence.

1956

Birth of AI

The Dartmouth Conference coins "Artificial Intelligence" as a field. McCarthy, Minsky, Rochester, and Shannon lay the groundwork.

1997

Deep Blue

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, demonstrating the power of search algorithms and specialized hardware.

2012

Deep Learning Revolution

AlexNet wins ImageNet competition, marking the beginning of the deep learning era powered by GPUs and large datasets.

2017

Transformers

"Attention Is All You Need" introduces the Transformer architecture, revolutionizing NLP and eventually all of AI.

Now

The Age of Foundation Models

Large language models, multimodal AI, and generative systems are transforming every industry and how we interact with technology.

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence is the simulation of human intelligence by machines. It encompasses learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding. AI systems can be narrow (specialized) or general (human-level across tasks).

Narrow AI General AI Superintelligence

Key Concepts

Core AI concepts include: Agents that perceive and act, State spaces for problem representation, Heuristics for efficient search, Knowledge representation, and Probabilistic reasoning under uncertainty.

Agents Search Reasoning

AI vs ML vs DL

AI is the broadest concept — machines mimicking intelligence. Machine Learning is a subset where machines learn from data. Deep Learning is a subset of ML using multi-layered neural networks for complex pattern recognition.

Hierarchy Subsets Distinctions
Chapter 02

Machine Learning

The science of getting computers to learn and act without being explicitly programmed. Explore the three paradigms of machine learning.

Supervised Learning

Learning from labeled examples. The algorithm learns a mapping from inputs to outputs by studying input-output pairs. Think of it as learning with an answer key.

f(x) → y Learn mapping from input x to label y
  • Classification — Predict discrete categories (spam/not spam, cat/dog)
  • Regression — Predict continuous values (price, temperature)

Key Algorithms

Linear Regression Regression

Fits a line to data to predict continuous outputs. Foundation of predictive modeling.

→
Logistic Regression Classification

Despite its name, used for classification. Uses sigmoid function to output probabilities.

→
Decision Trees Both

Tree-like model of decisions. Splits data based on feature values. Highly interpretable.

→
Random Forest Ensemble

Ensemble of decision trees. Reduces overfitting through bagging and feature randomization.

→
SVM Classification

Finds the optimal hyperplane that maximally separates classes. Effective in high dimensions.

→
KNN Both

Classifies based on the majority vote of k nearest neighbors. Simple yet powerful.

→

Unsupervised Learning

Discovering hidden patterns in data without labeled examples. The algorithm finds structure on its own — like organizing a library without a cataloguing system.

f(x) → structure Discover patterns in unlabeled data x
  • Clustering — Group similar data points (customer segments, image grouping)
  • Dimensionality Reduction — Compress features while preserving information
  • Association — Discover rules between variables (market basket analysis)

Key Algorithms

K-Means Clustering

Partitions data into K clusters by minimizing within-cluster variance. Fast and scalable.

→
DBSCAN Clustering

Density-based clustering that finds arbitrarily shaped clusters and identifies noise points.

→
PCA Reduction

Principal Component Analysis finds directions of maximum variance to reduce dimensions.

→
t-SNE Visualization

Reduces high-dimensional data to 2D/3D for visualization while preserving local structure.

→
Autoencoders Reduction

Neural networks that learn compressed representations by encoding and decoding data.

→
Apriori Association

Discovers frequent itemsets and association rules in transactional databases.

→

Reinforcement Learning

Learning through trial and error by interacting with an environment. An agent takes actions and receives rewards or penalties — like training a dog with treats.

s, a → r, s' State + Action yields Reward + Next State
  • Agent — The learner/decision-maker
  • Environment — What the agent interacts with
  • Policy — Strategy mapping states to actions
  • Reward Signal — Feedback from the environment

Key Algorithms

Q-Learning Value-Based

Learns action values (Q-values) to find the optimal policy without a model of the environment.

→
Deep Q-Network Value-Based

Combines Q-learning with deep neural networks. Famously mastered Atari games from raw pixels.

→
Policy Gradient Policy-Based

Directly optimizes the policy by following the gradient of expected reward.

→
PPO Policy-Based

Proximal Policy Optimization. Stable, reliable training used in robotics and RLHF for LLMs.

→
Actor-Critic Hybrid

Combines value estimation (critic) with policy optimization (actor) for stable learning.

→
AlphaGo/Zero MCTS + RL

Combined tree search with deep RL to master Go. AlphaZero learned from self-play alone.

Essential ML Concepts

⚙

Training & Testing

Split data into training (learn patterns), validation (tune hyperparameters), and test (evaluate performance) sets. Typical split: 70/15/15.

⚖

Bias-Variance Tradeoff

Bias: error from oversimplifying (underfitting). Variance: error from over-complexity (overfitting). The goal is the sweet spot between both.

⚡

Gradient Descent

Optimization algorithm that iteratively adjusts parameters to minimize a loss function. Variants: SGD, Adam, RMSProp. The backbone of model training.

⚓

Cross-Validation

K-fold cross-validation trains K models on different data splits to get a robust performance estimate. Reduces the chance of lucky/unlucky splits.

📈

Feature Engineering

The art of creating informative features from raw data. Often matters more than algorithm choice. Includes normalization, encoding, and selection.

🛠

Regularization

Techniques to prevent overfitting: L1 (Lasso) promotes sparsity, L2 (Ridge) penalizes large weights, Dropout randomly deactivates neurons during training.

Deep Learning

Neural networks with multiple layers that learn hierarchical representations. The driving force behind modern AI breakthroughs.

How Neural Networks Work

Data flows through layers of interconnected neurons. Each connection has a weight that's adjusted during training.

Input Layer
Hidden Layer 1
Hidden Layer 2
Output Layer
1
Forward Pass

Input data is multiplied by weights, summed, and passed through activation functions at each layer.

2
Loss Calculation

Compare the network's output to the expected result using a loss function (MSE, Cross-Entropy).

3
Backpropagation

Calculate gradients of the loss with respect to each weight using the chain rule, flowing backwards.

4
Weight Update

Adjust weights using gradient descent: w = w - lr * gradient. Repeat for thousands of iterations.

Chapter 04

Traditional AI Algorithms

Classical algorithms that form the foundation of AI. Many modern systems still rely on these elegant approaches.

Search Algorithms

Finding solutions by exploring a state space

BFS (Breadth-First Search)

Explores all nodes at the current depth before moving deeper. Guarantees shortest path in unweighted graphs. Uses a queue.

Complete Optimal

DFS (Depth-First Search)

Explores as deep as possible before backtracking. Memory efficient but may not find the shortest path. Uses a stack.

Complete* Not Optimal

A* Search

Uses f(n) = g(n) + h(n) — actual cost plus heuristic estimate. Optimal when heuristic is admissible (never overestimates). The gold standard.

Complete Optimal*

Greedy Best-First

Expands the node that appears closest to the goal using only the heuristic h(n). Fast but can be misled by the heuristic.

Incomplete Not Optimal

Optimization Algorithms

Finding the best solution from all feasible solutions

Genetic Algorithms

Inspired by evolution. Maintains a population of solutions that evolve through selection, crossover, and mutation over generations.

Metaheuristic Population-based

Simulated Annealing

Inspired by metallurgy. Accepts worse solutions with decreasing probability over time, allowing escape from local optima.

Metaheuristic Single-solution

Hill Climbing

Iteratively improves the solution by moving to the best neighboring state. Simple but prone to getting stuck in local optima.

Local Search Local Optima

Probabilistic Methods

Reasoning under uncertainty using probability theory

Bayesian Networks

Directed acyclic graphs representing probabilistic relationships. Enable efficient inference about uncertain events using Bayes' theorem.

Principled Graphical Model

Hidden Markov Models

Model sequences with hidden states. Used in speech recognition, bioinformatics, and temporal pattern recognition. Viterbi algorithm finds most likely sequence.

Sequential Temporal

Monte Carlo Methods

Use random sampling to estimate complex quantities. MCMC (Markov Chain Monte Carlo) samples from complex probability distributions.

Stochastic Sampling
Chapter 05

When to Use Each Algorithm

Choosing the right algorithm is half the battle. Here's a practical guide showing when each algorithm shines, with real-world examples.

Linear Regression Supervised

When you need to predict a continuous number and the relationship between variables is roughly linear.

🏠
House Price Prediction

Predict sale price from square footage, bedrooms, location. Each feature contributes additively to price.

📈
Sales Forecasting

Forecast next quarter revenue based on ad spend, seasonality, and historical trends.

⚗
Salary Estimation

Estimate expected salary from years of experience, education level, and job title.

+ Interpretable, fast, great baseline
- Fails on non-linear patterns
Logistic Regression Supervised

When you need yes/no classification with probability outputs and the decision boundary is roughly linear.

📧
Spam Detection

Classify email as spam or not spam based on word frequencies, sender reputation, and link count.

💳
Credit Approval

Approve or deny a loan based on credit score, income, and debt-to-income ratio. Outputs probability of default.

🩹
Disease Screening

Flag patients as at-risk/not-at-risk for diabetes based on blood sugar, BMI, age, and family history.

+ Gives probabilities, highly interpretable
- Can't capture complex non-linear boundaries
Decision Tree / Random Forest Supervised

When you need an interpretable model or have mixed data types (numbers + categories). Random Forest when you want higher accuracy.

💰
Fraud Detection

Flag fraudulent transactions using amount, location, time-of-day, merchant category. "If amount > $5k AND foreign AND nighttime → suspicious."

🧰
Medical Diagnosis

Diagnose conditions from symptoms: "If fever AND cough AND fatigue → likely flu." Doctors can understand and verify the logic.

👤
Customer Churn Prediction

Predict which users will cancel their subscription based on usage patterns, support tickets, and payment history.

+ Handles mixed data, no scaling needed, explainable
- Single trees overfit; forests lose interpretability
SVM (Support Vector Machine) Supervised

When you have a small-to-medium dataset with clear class separation, especially in high-dimensional spaces.

📝
Text Classification

Categorize documents or reviews into topics. SVMs excel with high-dimensional sparse text features (bag-of-words).

🧬
Gene Expression Analysis

Classify cancer types from gene data — thousands of features but only hundreds of samples. SVM thrives here.

✍
Handwriting Recognition

Classify handwritten digits or characters. Kernel trick maps pixel data into separable feature space.

+ Effective in high dimensions, robust with kernel trick
- Slow on large datasets, hard to interpret
KNN (K-Nearest Neighbors) Supervised

When you have a small dataset, want a simple baseline, and believe similar inputs should have similar outputs.

🎬
Recommendation Systems

"Users who liked similar movies also liked this." Find the K most similar users and recommend what they enjoyed.

🔎
Anomaly Detection

Detect unusual patterns — if a data point's neighbors are all very far away, it's likely an outlier (fraud, defect).

🌍
Imputing Missing Data

Fill in missing values by averaging the values of the most similar complete records in the dataset.

+ Zero training time, intuitive, no assumptions
- Slow at prediction time, suffers in high dimensions
K-Means Clustering Unsupervised

When you need to group unlabeled data into a known number of clusters based on similarity.

👥
Customer Segmentation

Group customers into segments (budget, mid-tier, premium) by spending habits, frequency, and preferences for targeted marketing.

🎨
Image Color Quantization

Reduce an image from millions of colors to K representative colors. Each pixel gets assigned to its nearest color centroid.

📍
Geographic Zoning

Cluster delivery addresses into zones for route optimization, or group cell towers for network planning.

+ Fast, scalable, easy to implement
- Must specify K, assumes spherical clusters
PCA (Principal Component Analysis) Unsupervised

When you have too many features and need to reduce dimensionality while keeping the most important information.

👀
Face Recognition Preprocessing

Reduce a 10,000-pixel face image to 150 "eigenfaces" — the principal components that capture the most variance between faces.

🔬
Genomic Data Analysis

Reduce 20,000+ gene expressions to a few principal components to visualize population clusters and genetic variation.

📊
Feature Denoising

Remove noise from sensor data by keeping only the top components and discarding low-variance (noisy) dimensions.

+ Speeds up training, removes noise, enables visualization
- Linear only, components lose interpretability
CNN (Convolutional Neural Network) Deep Learning

When your input is grid-like data — images, video, spectrograms — and spatial patterns matter.

📸
Image Classification

"Is this a cat or a dog?" CNNs learn hierarchical features: edges → textures → parts → objects automatically.

🩺
Medical Imaging (X-rays, MRI)

Detect tumors, fractures, or retinal disease from scans. Often matches or exceeds radiologist accuracy.

🚗
Self-Driving Cars (Perception)

Detect pedestrians, lane markings, traffic signs, and other vehicles from camera feeds in real-time.

+ State-of-art for images, automatic feature extraction
- Needs lots of data and GPU compute
RNN / LSTM Deep Learning

When your data is sequential and the order matters — time series, text, audio, or any temporal pattern.

📈
Stock Price Forecasting

Predict future prices from historical sequences. LSTMs remember long-term trends while tracking short-term volatility.

🎤
Speech Recognition

Convert spoken audio to text. Process audio frames sequentially, understanding context from what came before.

🎵
Music Generation

Generate melodies note-by-note. The network learns musical structure — scales, chords, rhythm patterns — from training songs.

+ Handles variable-length sequences, captures temporal patterns
- Slow to train, largely replaced by Transformers for text
Transformer Deep Learning

When you need to model long-range dependencies in text, code, images, or any sequence. The dominant architecture in modern AI.

💬
Chatbots & Assistants (GPT, Claude)

Generate human-like responses by attending to the full conversation context. Power behind ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini.

💻
Code Generation (Copilot)

Autocomplete code, generate functions from comments, translate between languages. Understands syntax + semantics.

🌐
Machine Translation

Translate between languages with near-human quality. Self-attention captures grammar relationships across the whole sentence.

+ Parallelizable, captures global context, state-of-art for NLP
- Extremely compute-intensive, needs massive data
GAN (Generative Adversarial Network) Deep Learning

When you need to generate realistic synthetic data — images, audio, or augment training datasets.

📸
Photorealistic Image Generation

Generate faces, artwork, or product photos that don't exist. StyleGAN creates high-res faces indistinguishable from real ones.

🎨
Style Transfer & Image Editing

Turn photos into paintings, swap seasons in landscapes, age/de-age faces, or convert sketches to realistic images.

📊
Training Data Augmentation

Generate synthetic medical images when real data is scarce. Create rare-case examples to balance imbalanced datasets.

+ Produces highly realistic outputs
- Hard to train (mode collapse), unstable
Q-Learning / DQN Reinforcement

When an agent must learn to make sequential decisions through trial-and-error in an environment with rewards.

🎮
Game Playing (Atari, Chess)

DQN mastered 49 Atari games from raw pixels. Learn which actions maximize score through millions of episodes.

🤖
Robot Navigation

Learn to navigate a warehouse, avoiding obstacles and finding optimal paths. Reward for reaching destination, penalty for collisions.

💡
Energy Management

Optimize HVAC systems in buildings. Agent learns when to heat/cool to minimize energy cost while maintaining comfort.

+ No labeled data needed, handles sequential decisions
- Needs many episodes, reward shaping is tricky
PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) Reinforcement

When you need stable, reliable RL training — especially for robotics, continuous control, or fine-tuning LLMs with RLHF.

🤖
Robotic Arm Control

Train a robotic arm to grasp objects, assemble parts, or pour liquids. Continuous action space (joint angles/torques).

💬
RLHF for LLMs

Fine-tune language models to be helpful, harmless, and honest. PPO optimizes the model based on human preference rewards.

🚶
Locomotion (Walking/Running)

Teach simulated or physical robots to walk, run, and balance. PPO handles the complex continuous action spaces well.

+ Stable training, works for continuous actions, industry standard
- Sample inefficient, sensitive to hyperparameters
A* Search Classical AI

When you need to find the optimal path in a graph or grid and have a good heuristic estimate of distance to goal.

🗺
GPS Navigation

Find shortest route between two locations on a road network. Heuristic: straight-line distance to destination.

🎮
Game Pathfinding (NPCs)

Move characters through game maps avoiding walls and obstacles. Used in virtually every video game with AI movement.

🧮
Puzzle Solving

Solve the 15-puzzle, Rubik's cube, or Sokoban. A* guarantees finding the optimal (fewest moves) solution.

+ Optimal + complete with admissible heuristic
- Memory-intensive for large state spaces
Genetic Algorithm Classical AI

When you need to optimize a complex function with no gradient — especially with large, discrete, or mixed search spaces.

📦
Scheduling & Logistics

Optimize delivery routes (TSP), staff schedules, or manufacturing job ordering. Evolve near-optimal schedules over generations.

✈
Engineering Design

Optimize wing shapes, antenna designs, or circuit layouts. NASA used GAs to evolve antenna designs that outperformed human engineers.

🧠
Neural Architecture Search

Evolve the structure of neural networks — how many layers, what connections, which activation functions — automatically.

+ No gradient needed, explores broadly, handles any objective
- Slow convergence, no optimality guarantee
Bayesian Networks Classical AI

When you need to reason about cause-and-effect under uncertainty and want interpretable probabilistic reasoning.

🩺
Clinical Decision Support

"Given symptoms X, Y, Z, what's the probability of disease A vs B?" Model causal relationships between symptoms, tests, and diseases.

⚠
Risk Assessment

Model cascading failure probabilities in systems — "If component A fails, what's the probability that system C goes down?"

📧
Spam Filtering (Naive Bayes)

Calculate P(spam | words). Simple Bayesian classifier that's fast, effective, and the basis of many email filters.

+ Interpretable, handles missing data, causal reasoning
- Requires domain expertise to build, computationally expensive for large networks
